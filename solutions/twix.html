Welcome to Twix!

Twix helps you get the most from your Box.com investment, delivering sophisticated approval workflows directly in Slack. We begin by asking you a few questions about your workflow needs, including the number of review steps and whether to send the document to a channel or user for review.

Slack team members can then submit documents directly in Slack, where Twix will orchestrate the process, running each step you define. Slack team members get real-time status updates as the document moves through the process. We prompt submitters to fix rejected documents and provide steps to remediate—all in Slack.

Once the process concludes, the documents are stored at Box.com, along with a full audit history of the process, including all document versions and approval steps. See what’s possible when Box and Slack meet in the middle with Twix.

FAQ

How do you compare to Box Relay?

We think Relay is an amazing workflow product. But we also feel it would be so much more powerful if it could run atop a messaging platform like Slack. Slack is a natural fit for role-based workflows and it’s hard to overlook just how efficient the approval process can be when paired with Slack channels and rich interactive messages.

How many approvals can I build?

You can design up to 5 document approval workflows. For example, you may choose to have a “timesheet approval” workflow, an “RFP approval” workflow, etc. It’s up to you to design what you need.

How many steps can an approval workflow have?

We allow up to 3 steps in each approval workflow you design. There is no limit for paid accounts.

What is the maximum document size allowed?

We support uploads up to 5MB. Paid accounts start at 20MB and can be configured for more.

How many approvals can I run each month?

You can run up to 20 free approvals each month. An approval includes all review steps as well as all necessary document resubmissions for rejected documents. If a submitter needs to fix and resubmit their document 10 times, that’s OK! (We'll feel sorry for you, but we won't mind at all.)

Can I assign a review tasks to both channels and users?

Yes, when you configure an approval workflow, you are prompted to choose whether the given approval step should be assigned to a user or a channel. If you choose to assign the review to a channel, you must invite Twix to the channel, so we can post messages to channel members, alerting them to claim and review the document. If you choose to assign the reivew to a user, we will prompt you to choose a named user on your team, or you can choose to dynamically assign the user based upon the profile of the submitter. For example, if your Slack instance tracks the manager email in a Slack profile field, we can dynamically route an approval to any individual's manager by reading this field during the approval process.

How much does it cost to upgrade if I need more resources?

Reach out to us at contact@intwixt.com if you’d like to pay for additional workflows. We’ll help you choose a plan that best meets your needs. Does your organization have a few end users who need to run a lot of workflows, or do you have a lot of end users running very few workflows? Tell us what you need, and we’ll help you find the plan that best meets your usage profile.

What happens when a document is rejected?

When a document is rejected, the original submitter is provided steps to fix the issue and is prompted to resubmit the updated document. We then upload the copy the document from Slack to Box and create a new document version in Box. Once the document review process successfully completes, the full audit history is available, including all submitted versions.

How many administrators can we have?

The free trial version allows one administrator. This individual must have access to the Box.com directories where the documents will reside during and after the review process. Administrators are prompted to grant access to Box.com upon installation of the Twix app. They can then design and configure approval workflows as needed. Administrators can add additional approval workflows (up to 5 total) by issuing the slash command, `twix /config`.

How many end users can we have?

We allow unlimited end users, including reviewers and submitters. Our usage meter only tracks the number of administrators (1) and the number of approval flows that run in a month (20) for trial accounts.

What private information do you keep on your servers?

We save your Slack Team ID and the Slack user ID for the administrator. We also track the number of approval flows run each month by your team. As an orchestration platform, we temporarily store the data that is transported through our system; however, all data is fully scrubbed from our servers within 30 minutes of the workflow completing. We never inspect the contents of the uploaded document and only track its size and file name.

Do you share information with outside parties?

No, never, and not at all. It’s our intent to efficiently move your documents from Slack to Box, call the necessary task assignment APIs, and then delete all traces of the transaction from our servers once the process completes. Our company (Intwixt) designs business process apps that run atop Slack. We built Twix to showcase our abilities as a general orchestration platform. We hope you love Twix, but we really hope you love Intwixt!

How do I uninstall Twix?

Send the slash command, `twix /uninstall`, and we’ll erase your profile from our servers. However, you must uninstall the Twix app according to Slack’s own documentation for full cleanup.

Slash Commands

Create Workflow /twix config

Design a new workflow for your team. (This command is only available to the administrator.)

Uninstall Twix /twix uninstall

Remove all workflows saved by Twix. If you wish to change administrators, issue this command to start fresh with a new admin. You can also issue this command immediately before uninstalling the Twix app for full cleanup of all saved information. (This command is only available to the administrator.)

Add Workflow /twix help

Get help for using Twix.

Begin Workflow

Starting a new document workflow is as easy as uploading a document to the Twix app. We'll show you a list of all workflows that your Twix administrator has configured to kick off the process.

If you're resubmitting a rejected document, make sure to upload the document to the same conversation thread, so we know that this is a resubmission and not an entirely approval workflow.
